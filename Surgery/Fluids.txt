A 95 kg patient sustains a 42% Total Body Surface Area (TBSA) burn. He received 3 liters of 
crystalloid before arrival, 4 hours post-burn. Using the Parkland formula, what is the hourly 
infusion rate for the remaining 4 hours of the first 8hour period?
 A) 800 mL/hr
 B) 1000 mL/hr
 C) 1250 mL/hr
 D) 1500 mL/hr
 E) 1800 mL/hr
Correct Answer: C) 1250 mL/hr
Explanation: 1. Total fluid 24h = 4 mL x 95 kg x 42 = 15,960 mL. 2. First 8h requirement (50%) = 7,980 mL. 
3. Fluid already given = 3,000 mL. 4. Remaining fluid for first 8h = 4,980 mL. 5. Time remaining in first 8h 
window = 4 hours. 6. Rate = 4,980 mL / 4 hr = 1,245 mL/hr (closest to 1250).

A 70-year-old male sustains a high-voltage electrical burn. On admission, his urine is dark 
in color, and urine output is 30 mL/hr. Compartment pressure in the affected limb is 20 mmHg. 
What is the main complication that should be anticipated and treated in this patient?
 A) Acute renal failure
 B) Cardiac arrhythmia
 C) Compartment syndrome requiring fasciotomy
 D) Curling's ulcer
 E) Pulmonary embolism
Correct Answer: A) Acute renal failure
Explanation: Dark urine after a high-voltage electrical burn suggests myoglobinuria due to rhabdomyolysis. 
This places the patient at high risk of acute kidney injury and requires prompt fluid resuscitation and 
monitoring.

Large volume resuscitation with 0.9% Normal Saline is associated with which acid-base 
disturbance?
 A) Metabolic alkalosis
 B) Respiratory acidosis
 C) Hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis
 D) Hypochloremic metabolic acidosis
 E) Respiratory alkalosis
Correct Answer: C) Hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis
Explanation: Normal saline contains high levels of chloride (154 mEq/L) compared to plasma. Excessive 
administration leads to hyperchloremia and a normal anion gap metabolic acidosis.
